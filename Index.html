What You Should Know About Credit Cards

A credit card lets you borrow money from a bank to make purchases. Unlike a debit card, which uses money directly from your bank account, a credit card uses borrowed money that must be paid back later.


Credit cards can help build your credit history when used responsibly, but late payments and overspending can lead to debt and lower credit scores.

How Credit Cards Work

When you use a credit card, the bank pays for your purchase first. At the end of the billing cycle, you receive a statement showing how much you owe.


If you pay the full balance by the due date, you can often avoid interest charges. Paying late may result in fees and additional costs.

How To Gain Credit Score

Building a strong credit score takes time and responsible financial habits.


• Pay bills on time
• Keep balances low
• Use credit responsibly
• Avoid opening too many cards at once
• Check your credit report regularly

How To Use Credit Cards Responsibly

Only spend money you know you can repay. Treat your credit card like a tool, not free money.


Making payments on time and staying within your budget helps avoid debt and supports a healthy credit score.

Common Credit Card Mistakes

Avoid these common mistakes:


• Paying only the minimum balance
• Missing payment deadlines
• Maxing out your credit card
• Spending more than you can afford
• Applying for too many cards at once


These actions can increase debt and negatively affect your credit score.

Safety Tips & Fraud Prevention

Protect your financial information and stay alert for scams.


• Never share your PIN or card details
• Use strong passwords
• Monitor your account regularly
• Shop only on secure websites
• Report suspicious activity immediately


Practicing safe online habits can help prevent fraud and identity theft.
